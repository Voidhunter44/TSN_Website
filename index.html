About Me

Hi, welcome. My name is Yousha Ahmed.

I’m a 14-year-old creator, builder, and thinker from Boston, originally from Pakistan. I’m a student in the Engineering Pathway at John D. O’Bryant School of Math & Science, and I love combining logic, design, and storytelling to make cool things that actually work.

What I’m Passionate About

I’m passionate about building things that people can interact with like games, tools, or visual experiences. I love the creative side of design and the problem solving side of code. To me, there's something powerful about starting with nothing and ending up with a working project.

I’m especially drawn to:

  • Low poly 3D game assets
  • Clean mobile apps
  • Physics and real-world logic
  • Learning how tech connects to people’s lives

What I Do (My Niche)

I design and build projects that sit between games, design, and development. My niche is:

  • Creating low poly game environments and assets that are stylized, lightweight, and fun
  • Developing apps using Flutter for smooth mobile experiences
  • Writing code in Python and Dart to automate or solve real problems
  • Using Blender and Canva to visualize or prototype my ideas

Whether I’m building a character rig, a timer app, or a mini physics-based game, I focus on making things that are simple, useful, and creative.

Why I Do It (Short Story)

When I was younger, I used to take apart remote controls and try to "fix" them (spoiler: I usually broke them). But I never stopped wondering how things worked. That same energy drives me today but now I’ve leveled up with real tools and real purpose.

I still ask, “What if I try this?” but now the answer sometimes turns into a working app, a model, or a concept people can actually use. I do it because I love that moment when something finally works and I know I built it myself.

What Makes Me Unique

  • I care about the small details that others skip like background shadows, load times, or how a user feels using something.
  • I don’t just focus on code or looks — I aim for the balance between creative and functional.
  • I'm young, but I’m not afraid to go deep into tools and learn things most people my age haven’t touched yet.
  • I keep improving whether it’s adding tiny changes to my workout routine or making my app UI one pixel at a time.
